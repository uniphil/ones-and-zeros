Choosing a power supply

Printer Power will work well with most DC adapters between 9 and 24 volts. If you have box of old power adapters kicking around, chances are that one of them will work!

Voltage

Printer Power works well because it uses a switching DC-DC converter to trade voltage ("volts" or just "V") for more current ("amps" or just "A"). It's pre-configured to provide your printer with 7V regardless of the input voltage, but this can be adjusted if you need it.

Connecting your printer

CSN-A2 Printer

The printers come with two cables: a 2-wire black/red cable for power ("DC IN"), and a 3-wire black/yellow/green cable for serial communication ("TTL").

Connect both cables to your printer and to Printer Power. That's it! You can now run the diagnostic test.

Adafruit Nano and JP Panel Thermal Receipt Printer

Nano printers (from Adafruit) and JP Panel printers (most cheap Chinese models) come with a single 5-pin connector.

Connect it to the 5-pin connector on Printer Power and you're all set to run the diagnostic test!
